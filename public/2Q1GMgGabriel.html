University of the Philippines

History, Vision and Mission, and Undergraduate Programs for STEM Students

History

The University of the Philippines (UP) was founded on June 18, 1908, as a way to fulfill the growing need of the people for an opportunity to pursue studies in medicine, law, engineering, and/or applied sciences. UP is rich in history—from having to transfer campuses post-WWII to battles on campus between UP students protesting police brutality and policemen during former president Ferdinand Marcos' Martial Law. Through it all, UP continues to be a shining example of its core values: Honor, Excellence, and Service to the Nation.

Vision and Mission

A great university, taking a leadership role in the development of a globally competitive Philippines. Driven by:
Academic excellence and operational excellence;
Strong research and creative capability, supported by an expanded graduate program and geared to addressing the country’s problems;
Excellent faculty and staff working in an environment conducive to outstanding performance and high productivity;
The best and brightest students from across the country prepared for successful careers and responsive citizenship;
Strong support from the alumni and other stakeholders;
High visibility and effective public service;
Modernized physical facilities and technological infrastructure for teaching, research and administration; and
Financial sustainability achieved by resource generation and administrative efficiency, while preserving its public character.

UP Hymn – "UP Naming Mahal"

The University of the Philippines Hymn, "UP Naming Mahal" or "UP Beloved" was composed by Nicanor Abelardo with lyrics by Ildefonso Santos. It is traditionally sung during official university events and ceremonies, embodying the spirit and pride of the UP community.
